Overview

Hotels frequently misprice rooms and misforecast revenue because they rely on historical assumptions instead of data. This project analyzed the public Hotel Booking Demand dataset (119,390 bookings, City & Resort hotels, 2015–2017) to identify what actually drives booking value, then built two complementary regression models to explain revenue and pricing behaviour separately.

Data & preparation

  • Sourced from Kaggle's Hotel Booking Demand dataset — 119,390 observations, 32 original variables.
  • Cleaned missing values contextually (e.g. blank "children" imputed as 0), removed the low-value "agent" column, and capped ADR outliers via winsorization (0.001–0.999 quantiles) after finding a single unrealistic ₦5,400/night record.
  • Engineered new features: booking ID, guest composition segments (single / two-adult / group / family), lead-time categories, room-change flag, room-nights, and a derived Revenue target (ADR × room nights).
  • Restricted analysis to confirmed, non-cancelled bookings and removed logically inconsistent records, refining the dataset to 73,167 observations.

Analysis & modelling

  • Ran univariate, bivariate, and multivariate EDA across booking characteristics, pricing, stay duration, seasonality, and geography, plus a SQL layer for business-question queries.
  • Built a correlation matrix to detect multicollinearity before modelling (room_nights and stays_in_week_nights were 0.94 correlated).
  • Trained two Linear Regression models to avoid data leakage: Model 1 predicted total Revenue directly (R² 0.86, RMSE 120.4) — strong but partly inflated since ADR/room-nights are structurally embedded in revenue. Model 2 predicted ADR independently (R² 0.52, RMSE 31.94) to isolate genuine pricing drivers from volume effects.

Key findings

  • Two-adult transient travellers generate 67.6% of total revenue despite paying below-average rates — the hotel's revenue is volume-driven, not price-driven.
  • ADR is shaped mainly by room type, summer seasonality, and direct/OTA booking channel — not by customer type or geography.
  • Family and group travellers pay ~31% higher average daily rates but are underrepresented, flagging them as a high-value growth segment.
  • Summer (Jun–Aug) drives 34.5% of total room-nights and the highest monthly revenue; Portugal, the UK, and France are the dominant source markets.
